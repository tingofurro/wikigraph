In music , a major fourth ( Play ( help · info ) ) is the interval from the quarter tone scale, named by Ivan Wyschnegradsky , between the perfect fourth (500 cents) and the augmented fourth (600 cents) and thus 550 cents (F ). It inverts to a minor fifth .

Just augmented fourth on C.

The term may also be applied to the ratio 45:32 (F ♯ +) or 590.22 cents ( Play ( help · info ) ), formed from the major third (5/4 = 386.31) and the major tone (9/8 = 203.91), [ 1 ] which is flat of the ET tritone , which is 600 cents.
